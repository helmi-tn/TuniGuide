MOST POLPULAR CITES IN TUNISIA

Ribat

Sitting right on Monastir's shorefront, the Ribat is the town's most famous monument. The imposing fortress was built by Harthama ben Ayan in AD 796.

Habib Bourguiba Mausoleum

The mausoleum was built while Bourguiba was still alive, in 1963

Bourguiba Mosque

The beautiful mosque was originally built in 1963.

As the birthplace of Tunisia's favorite son - Habib Bourguiba, founder of the Tunisian Republic - Monastir is home to a grand mosque in tribute to the ex-president.

Medina

Founded in 960 BC by the Carthaginians

The souk streets of Monastir's medina (Old Town) area have been spruced up and modernized, which has, unfortunately, brushed away much of the area's historic charm.

Cemetery

The cemetery was made between 1916 and 1920

Immediately northwest of the Ribat lies Monastir's atmospheric cemetery, which holds a vast number of beautiful old tombs. Many are decorated with bands of Kufic inscriptions and faience tiles.

Harbor

The imposing fortress was built by Harthama ben Ayan in AD 796

Monastir's pretty seafront promenade (known as the Corniche) leads to the Port de Plaisance harbor, where pleasure yachts bob jauntily on the Mediterranean.

El Djem

El Djem has been settled since the 3rd century BC,

For most foreign tourists, a holiday in Monastir wouldn't be complete without a day trip to the mighty El Djem amphitheater, an easy 60-kilometer drive away from town.

Monastir Beaches

Monastir. If you're looking for just a tad more adventure and action, Monastir beach will keep you going long after sunset

For many visitors, Monastir's main attraction lies in its strips of perfect white sand. The town itself hugs the coastline, surrounded by beaches stretching along the coast in both directions

Skanes

The northern suburb of Skanès (just off Route de la Falaise) is packed full of gorgeous, dazzlingly-white villas and luxury hotels that have incorporated traditional Tunisian architecture into their mid-century modern architectural design.
